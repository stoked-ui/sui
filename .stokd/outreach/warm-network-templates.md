# Warm-Network Outreach — Templates

Goal: reactivate dormant relationships and ask for **intros**, not work. The free AI Readiness Audit is your foot-in-the-door — easy yes for them to forward to a contact who needs it.

**Rules of engagement**
- Personalize the first sentence. Reference something specific you remember. Generic openers get ignored.
- Keep it short. 3-5 short paragraphs max.
- Ask for **one** specific thing. Not "let me know if you can help" — "do you know anyone at X who runs Y?"
- Lead with what's in it for the person you're messaging (catch up, you'd love to hear about their work) — not with what's in it for you.
- Don't be apologetic. You're offering something valuable for free.

## Variants on the offer (test what works)

The free audit framing is the core. Variants if your initial sends get a "what specifically?" reply:

- **"60-min Discovery + Audit"** — more formal, better for ex-managers
- **"Three free audits this month — first-come"** — scarcity framing, works on operators
- **"AI Coffee — 30 min over Zoom + a written follow-up"** — softer, works on intros from cold contacts
- **"Free pilot build — 1 week of my time on your top AI opportunity, no commitment"** — bigger swing, only offer to high-fit prospects

---

## Don'ts

- **Don't BCC.** Every message is individual.
- **Don't attach your resume.** This is a peer ask, not a job hunt.
- **Don't follow up more than once.** If they don't reply after a 7-10 day nudge, drop it. The well is deep.
- **Don't message during business hours on Mondays or Fridays.** Tues-Thurs, 9-11am Central is the sweet spot.
- **Don't sound desperate.** You're offering free expert work — they're getting a favor by accepting it.

---

## Tracking

Set up a simple sheet (Notion, Airtable, Google Sheets, or just a `outreach.csv`) with: name, channel, sent date, replied (y/n), action (audit booked / intro made / passed / no response). Review weekly. After 30 messages, look at the response rate — if it's under 20%, the templates aren't landing; iterate the first sentence.

A 1-in-5 response rate is healthy. A 1-in-10 audit-booked rate from responses is healthy. So 30 messages → ~6 replies → 1-2 audits booked. Repeat next week.
